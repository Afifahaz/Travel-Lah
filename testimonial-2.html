The Silver Valley

In the Kampar area, Gopeng is a quiet town that can be reached by driving in about 30 minutes from Ipoh and in about 2 hours from Kuala Lumpur. It is without a doubt the go-to location for anyone to jump, swim, trek, and float into an adventure since it is framed by magnificent limestone cliffs, rivers, and caves. Along with the Rafflesia, the biggest flower in the world, and the endangered Rajah Brooke Butterfly, its ancient jungles are also home to the indigenous Semai people.

Gopeng's reputation is undoubtedly dominated by nature, but the little town also possesses a number of cultural landmarks. Gopeng, which once had a substantial tin mining industry, drew a sizable Chinese population, whose culture is still deeply ingrained in the town's ancient pre-war structures.
